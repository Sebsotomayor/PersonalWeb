About Me

I'm Sebastian Sotomayor

I'm a Computer Engineering student at the University of Central Florida with a strong interest in building systems that bridge hardware and software. I enjoy working across the full stack from embedded firmware and low level communication to backend services and user facing interfaces with a focus on reliability, maintainability, and clear system design.

My technical experience includes programming in C and C++, as well as working with Python and Java in larger software systems. I've developed and debugged software on Linux based environments, collaborated within Agile style workflows, and applied engineering standards and structured methodologies to solve complex problems. I'm especially interested in systems where performance, determinism, and correctness matter.

At UCF, I actively contribute to team driven engineering projects and technical communities. As a member of the IEEE student branch, I worked on the electrical team responsible for PCB design for UCF’s autonomous robot in the IEEE SoutheastCon 2025 hardware competition, where our team earned second place among nearly 60 universities. This experience strengthened my ability to collaborate across disciplines and contribute effectively in small, focused teams.

Beyond academics and professional experience, I enjoy building projects that combine technical depth with creativity. Inspired by organizations such as Nintendo and Disney, my goal is to design technology driven experiences that are intuitive, engaging, and meaningful whether through software, hardware, or a blend of both.
