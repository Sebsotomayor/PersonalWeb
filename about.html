About Me

I'm Sebastian Sotomayor

I am a Computer Engineering student at the University of Central Florida with a passion for solving problems at the intersection of hardware and software. My experience spans embedded systems, algorithms, and full-stack development, where I've worked with microcontrollers like the MSP430FR6989, developed enterprise simulators in Java, and built applications using both MERN and LAMP stacks. These projects have strengthened my ability to deliver impactful and efficient solutions.

At UCF, I actively contribute to technical communities. As part of the IEEE student branch, I worked on the electrical team that designed the PCB for UCF's autonomous robot at IEEE SoutheastCon 2025, earning a second-place finish among nearly 60 teams. I've also been involved with Hack@UCF, where I explore cybersecurity and problem-solving in fast-paced, team-driven environments.

Beyond academics, I enjoy creating projects that merge technology with creativity. Inspired by organizations like Nintendo and Disney, my goal is to build meaningful experiences—through software, hardware, or both—that leave a lasting impact.
