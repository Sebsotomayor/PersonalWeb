Projects

IEEE SoutheastCon 2025 Student Hardware Competition

Overview:

The IEEE SoutheastCon Hardware Competition is a student-centered program focused on giving college students a unique and exciting learning opportunity with robotics. Each year, teams take on a new game where they design, build, test, and program a fully Autonomous Robot to perform a series of tasks. This year, the objective is to create a fully autonomous robot that is capable of moving, locating, pick up objects, and place those objects at a specified location

Prompt:

The year is 2047. After GRID deflected asteroids from comet 2047-5-L1, valuable materials—Geodinium and Nebulite—were discovered within them. Your rover team has been tasked with collecting these rare Astral Materials before the asteroids drift back into space forever. You have T-3 minutes to gather as much as possible, load them into cosmic shipping containers, and deliver them to the designated rendezvous pad. Telemetry reports also hint at a mysterious cave nearby. The materials your rover collects will pave the way for groundbreaking new technologies, shaping the future of humanity!

Progress so far:

I am part of the Electrical Team for this project, responsible for powering the autonomous robot and its various components. At the start of the semester, we practiced using the circuit simulation tool LTspice to strengthen our skills. As a team, we have conducted research on circuit elements such as buck converters and voltage dividers, which will be implemented into the robot's design. Additionally, we have tested components from last year's robot to assess their functionality and determine whether they can be reused.

This project is ongoing
